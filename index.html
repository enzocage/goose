How to play

Roll your cube across the world to reach the glowing green Exit. Collect every Prism along the way. Don't roll into hazards or off the edge into the void — press R to restart anytime.

Controls

WASD / ←↑↓→ — roll (hold to repeat every 300 ms)
E — place block in front at standing height (costs 1 block limit)
Q — place block in front 1 level higher (act as a stair/step)
R — restart level
Middle Click — x-ray view: makes blocks see-through so you can reveal the whole 3D structure hidden behind walls
P — pause & free-orbit camera: drag to rotate 360° around your cube (always centred), wheel or pinch to zoom — works with mouse & touch
H — toggle this help
♪ icon (bottom-left) — background music player
Load Level / Editor (top) — pick a level or build your own

Level editor

Click Editor (top bar) to build your own level. Pick a block or item from the left Toolbox, then place it on the grid. Set the spawn (Start) and goal (Exit), and use Playtest to try your level — Exit returns to the editor.

Left Click — place / paint the selected tool · drag to paint a row
Hold V + Drag Left Click — draw a rectangular plane (width & length) of blocks; release V or mouse to commit
Right Click — erase (tap) · Right Drag — orbit the camera
WASD / QE — pan & rotate the editor camera · wheel — zoom
R / F — raise / lower the editing height · Shift+wheel or the right-side ruler also change height
1…90 — quick-pick tools · X — eraser · L — linker
Linker — connect a switch/plate ➜ its bridge/platform, or pair two teleporters (source then target, Esc to cancel)
Hold O — group every block you place into one compound object (moves together); release to finish
U or Ctrl+Z — undo (up to 250 steps) · Slice — hide blocks above the current height for easier editing

AI level generators

In the editor, pick a generator from the dropdown and press Generate. Every AI Pro level is guaranteed solvable.

AI Gen · Labyrinth — a quick randomised 3D maze.
AI Pro · Difficulty — a guaranteed-solvable level scaled to a difficulty from 1 (very easy) to 10 (extreme), using all element types.
AI Pro2 · Criteria — tick the design criteria you want (verticality, teleporters, crates, hazards, enemies …); a size slider scales the amount.
AI Pro3 · Quantified — like Pro2, but set the exact quantity for each of 20 criteria, plus tuning knobs (path length, arena size, par strictness, platform speed …).

Save, load & share levels

Name your level and pick a Theme in the top bar, then:

Save — store the level in your browser (localStorage) under its name.
Load — open the library panel and pick any saved level (scroll the list); the built-in Demo showcases every element.
Export — view the level as JSON to copy, or Save File to download a .json.
Import — paste level JSON or Load File to open a downloaded .json.
The campaign levels load from level/1.json, 2.json, … — finish one to roll into the next.
